Frequently Asked Questions

There are two ways to rent a dumpster from Dumpsters.com. If you know exactly what you need, order your dumpster online in just a few clicks. If you'd like expert support when choosing your dumpster size and weight limit, give our team a call and they'll help you book the best dumpster for your project.

We offer 10-40 yard dumpster sizes to ensure there's always a dumpster for your project. For smaller projects, or those with heavy debris like roofing shingles or concrete, we recommend a 10 yard dumpster. For mid-size projects like attic and garage cleanouts, a 20 yard dumpster is our most popular size. A 30 or 40 yard dumpster is the best option for big jobs, like whole-home cleanouts, large construction, demolition and more.

We keep things simple with an upfront, flat-rate estimate that includes dumpster delivery, rental period, pickup, debris disposal and a set weight limit ‚Äî everything you need to get started. When we pick up your dumpster, if it weighs more than your weight limit, you'll pay a clearly explained, pro-rated fee for the additional weight. No hidden charges, ever.

You usually don't need a permit if you're planning to keep your dumpster on private property, like a driveway or jobsite. If you'd like to keep your dumpster on the street or other public property, you may need a dumpster permit from your local municipality.

Our typical dumpster rental period is between seven and 10 days. If you don't need a dumpster that long, you can always schedule pickup early. If you need to extend your rental, just let us know. You can usually hold onto your roll off dumpster for a flat daily rate.
