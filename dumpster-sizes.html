Weight Guidelines & Material Types

Understanding how different materials affect your dumpster weight limit

Construction Debris

Average weight: 1,500-2,000 lbs/yard

  • Wood, drywall, flooring
  • Insulation, tiles
  • Light fixtures, hardware
Pro tip: 20-yard ideal for most construction projects

Concrete & Masonry

Average weight: 4,000-4,500 lbs/yard

  • Concrete, bricks, stone
  • Ceramic tiles, pavers
  • Stucco, mortar
Pro tip: Use 10-yard for heavy materials like concrete

Roofing Materials

Average weight: 2,500-4,500 lbs/square

  • Asphalt shingles (3 bundles/sq)
  • Wood shakes, metal roofing
  • Underlayment, flashing
Pro tip: 10-yard per 20-25 squares of shingles

Yard Waste & Landscaping

Average weight: 500-1,000 lbs/yard

  • Branches, shrubs, grass clippings
  • Dirt, soil, mulch
  • Stumps, rocks, gravel
Pro tip: 20-yard for moderate yard cleanups

Household Junk

Average weight: 200-400 lbs/yard

  • Furniture, appliances
  • Toys, decorations, books
  • General household items
Pro tip: 20-yard perfect for whole-home cleanouts

Scrap Metal

Average weight: 1,000-3,000 lbs/yard

  • Steel, aluminum, copper
  • Piping, ductwork, fixtures
  • Automotive parts, equipment
Pro tip: Heavier than household junk, plan accordingly
