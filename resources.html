Dumpster Sizing

The right size depends on your project scope. Use our size guide for detailed recommendations. Generally: 10-yard for small projects (garage cleanouts, small roofing), 20-yard for medium projects (whole-home cleanouts, kitchen remodels), 30-40 yards for large construction and demolition projects.

Placement & Permits

You typically don't need a permit if you're placing the dumpster on private property (driveway, yard). However, if you need to place it on public property (street, sidewalk, or public right-of-way), you'll likely need a municipal permit. Check our permit guide for location-specific requirements.

Ideal placement is on a flat, hard surface like concrete or asphalt. Driveways are most common. Avoid soft ground, grass, or areas that could be damaged. Consider easy access for loading, clearance for overhead obstacles, and protection for surfaces (we can provide boards if needed).

While modern trucks are gentle, there's always some risk to surfaces. We recommend placing plywood boards under the dumpster to distribute weight and protect your driveway. Ask about our driveway protection options when ordering.

Prohibited Items

Prohibited items typically include: hazardous materials (paints, chemicals, solvents), batteries, tires, electronics, appliances with Freon (refrigerators, AC units), medical waste, asbestos, and certain chemicals. See our complete waste guidelines for detailed lists.

Liquid paint and hazardous chemicals cannot go in dumpsters. For latex paint: let it dry completely (add kitty litter or sawdust to speed drying), then dispose of the dried paint and empty cans. Oil-based paints and all hazardous chemicals require special disposal at household hazardous waste facilities.

Dumpster Permit Guide

Understanding when and how to get dumpster placement permits

When Do You Need a Permit?

No Permit Required

  • On private property (driveway, yard, private land)
  • In business parking lots with owner permission
  • On construction sites with proper site control

Permit Typically Required

  • On public streets or sidewalks
  • In public right-of-way areas
  • Blocking any traffic lanes or paths

How to Get a Permit

1

Check Local Requirements

Contact your city or county public works department

2

Submit Application

Typically includes location, duration, and dumpster size

3

Pay Permit Fee

Fees range from $25-$150+ depending on location

4

Display Permit

Keep permit visible on dumpster during rental period

Permit Tips & Considerations

Apply Early: Permit processing can take 3-10 business days
Duration Limits: Most permits are for 7-30 days maximum
Location Restrictions: Some areas prohibit street placement entirely
Insurance Requirements: Some cities require proof of insurance
HOA Rules: Check homeowner association rules even for private property

Project Tips & Best Practices

Expert advice to make your dumpster rental experience smooth and efficient

üè† Home Renovation Projects

Preparation: Clear the delivery path and protect surfaces with plywood boards
Loading Strategy: Start with heavier items at the bottom, lighter items on top
Space Management: Break down large items (furniture, cabinets) to maximize space
Safety: Never overfill - keep waste level with or below container top edge

üèóÔ∏è Construction & Demolition

Heavy Materials: Consider 10-yard dumpsters for heavy items like concrete
Multiple Phases: Schedule multiple deliveries if project has distinct phases
Site Access: Ensure clear 12-foot wide path for truck access
Weight Distribution: Spread heavy materials evenly, don't concentrate in one area

üçÉ Yard & Landscaping

Green Waste: Allow extra space as organic materials compress over time
Dirt & Rock: Heavy soil and rocks may require separate, smaller containers
Branches: Cut large branches into 4-6 foot sections for easier loading
Seasonal: Fall leaf cleanup often requires larger containers than expected

üè¢ Commercial Projects

After-Hours: Schedule delivery/pickup outside business hours when possible
Parking Management: Use cones or signage to prevent unauthorized use
Security: Consider locks or gated areas for sensitive commercial sites
Compliance: Ensure all materials comply with commercial disposal regulations

Smart Loading Techniques

Layer Smart

Flat items at bottom, bulky items middle, small items fill gaps

Break Down Large Items

Furniture, boxes, and materials take less space when disassembled

Balance Weight

Distribute heavy items evenly to avoid shifting during transport

Fill All Spaces

Small items and bags can fill gaps between larger objects
